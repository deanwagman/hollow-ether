# The Courts

The **Courts** are the four great factions of the spirit world in Hollow Ether. Each court embodies a domain of change—how reality in the Hollow Ether is allowed to move. Spirits belong to a court; mortals do not, but they walk the threads between them.

They are **not** governments with elections or kings. Think of them as **philosophies with power**: each court maintains certain kinds of truth and refuses others.

## The four courts

| Court | Domain | Typical spirits | What they protect |
|-------|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Court of Ether** | Signal, connection, meaning across distance | Luminia | That messages stay true; the Hollow Ether stays readable |
| **Court of Tide** | Memory, reflection, what was felt but unspoken | Elara | That the past is not rewritten without consent |
| **Court of Gale** | Voice, oath, message in motion | Zephyr | That spoken promises bind; heralds may speak |
| **Court of Root** | Endings, decay, closure, cost | Calyx | That nothing false is preserved forever |

## How courts interact

- **No single court rules all.** Disputes (e.g. Elara’s hearing) are argued before mixed witnesses or informal consensus—not one supreme judge.
- **The Hollow Ether** is shared infrastructure; Ether court tends it, but all courts send traffic through it.
- **The Portal of Thalos** is a shared danger: opening it early would distort all four domains. Courts disagree on *when* it may open, not whether it exists.

## Why this matters for play

- Summoning a spirit invokes their court’s “laws” in dialogue (Tide spirits ask about memory; Root spirits ask what you’re willing to let die).
- Quest flags often record **which court you sided with** or offended.
- Visual mood can shift toward a court’s palette when their influence is high (e.g. Root → darker, compost-gold).

## Chapter 1 scope

Only these four courts appear, but **not at once**—the player meets one court’s spirit per act (see `lore/chapters/chapter-01.md`). Other courts or exiles may be named later as the Zephyr mystery deepens.
